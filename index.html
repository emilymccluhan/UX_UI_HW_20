Gamers Outreach Case Study

Non-Profit Redesign for 501(c3)nonprofit Gamers Outreach

The UC Berkeley UX/UI Bootcamp tasked my team of 5 with conducting a pro-bono redesign for a non-profit of our choice. We worked quickly within a 4 week time constraint, choosing to focus on making it easier for users to conduct fundraisers for Gamers Outreach.

Research and Definition

Problem

We needed to enhance the Gamers Outreach website in order to encourage more charity streams and foster positive reviews from, and thus build the trust of, the gaming community.

Solution

We re-designed the Gamers Outreach website to provide individuals with more accurate and meaningful information about fundraising efforts, and make it easier to support the nonprofit. This would help establish trust and confidence in the non-profit, increase awareness about fundraising efforts, and ultimately empower more individuals with the tools to get involved.

Ideation & Prototyping
After anaylzing our findings from initial research we began drafting wireframes that later turned into our initial low fidelity prototype for this project.
Testing
Inevitably, issues arose during our second round of usability testing, in which myself and another teammate conducted in-person usability tests of our mid-fidelity prototype. Many users could access the fundraising page, but not many though to click on the "about Twitch streaming" page that we developed. Through additional testing we were able to narrow down the issue to our button text. The initial label "About Twitch" was too vague, and users already familiar with Twitch's platform did not feel the need to click on the page, though they still felt they lacked clarity about setting up a fundraising stream with Twitch after scrolling through the fundraising page. Our solution was to put more active language in the button text, and changed the label to "Stream with Twitch"
Final
I would love to go back and build out solutions to other design flaws that were uncovered during our initial user interviews. We found there was difficulty for healthcare workers looking to request Gamer's Outreach services, but unfortunately we were unable to address this in our redesign given the time constraints.
